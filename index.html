Happiness Domains

USA Happiness Domains: <45 and 45+

Percent of responses mentioning topic

We asked 5,000 people what makes them happy. Answers were sweeping in nature, ranging from 'making a positive contribution to society' to simply 'parrots.' However, certain topics came up again and again. Worth noting that although the question was "what makes you happy?", respondents would occasionally answer in the negative/hypothetical: such as 'if I had more money, I would be more happy' or 'whenever I'm able to pay my bills on time.' This is important for the 'Finances' piece, since those who mention money were actually less happy than respondents not mentioning it. We do not see a similar result for 'family' or 'religion,' which is less often mentioned in the negative (I did see a 'when I don't have to be around my son'). Respondents mentioning family/religion are indeed happier than those not mentioning those topics.

Family was mentioned in 45 percent of responses, making it by far the most common theme.

Other common themes were health, finances, and religion, mentioned in 6.3, 6.2, and 4.5 percent of responses, respectively.

Something about age differences. Respondents 45+ were more likley to mention family, health, and religion than respondents younger than 45. Younger respondents were more likely to mention finances: making money, paying bills, their debt, the economy.
